Tim Berners-Lee

Inventor of the World Wide Web

About the Legend

☛ Tim Berners-Lee, born on June 8, 1955, in London, England, is a British computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web.

☛ He attended Queen's College, Oxford, where he received a first-class degree in physics in 1976.

☛ In 1989, while working at CERN, he proposed a project to create a distributed information system to facilitate information sharing among researchers.

☛ He developed the first web browser and web server, and on December 25, 1990, the first website was launched.

☛ The World Wide Web was made publicly available in 1991, revolutionizing how information is accessed and shared globally.

☛ Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) in 1994 to develop web standards and ensure the web's long-term growth.

☛ He has received numerous awards and honors, including being knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 2004 for his pioneering work.

☛ In 2012, he was named among the 100 most influential people in the world by Time magazine.

☛ He continues to advocate for an open and accessible internet, emphasizing the importance of privacy and net neutrality.

☛ Tim Berners-Lee's invention of the World Wide Web has had a profound impact on society, transforming communication, commerce, education, and entertainment.

☛ His vision of a free and open web remains a guiding principle for the ongoing development and governance of the internet.
